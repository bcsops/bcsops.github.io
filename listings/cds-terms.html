Definitions:

Credit Default Swap (CDS): A financial contract where the Buyer of the CDS makes periodic payments to the Seller in exchange for protection against the default of a specified Reference Entity.

Reference Entity: The company, sovereign, or other entity whose credit risk is being transferred under this agreement. It is the entity whose default or other credit events trigger a payout under the CDS.

Credit Event: A predefined event that affects the creditworthiness of the Reference Entity, including but not limited to:

  • Bankruptcy
  • Failure to Pay
  • Restructuring
  • Obligation Acceleration
Occurrence of a Credit Event leads to the Buyer’s right to receive compensation from the Seller.

Notional Amount: The face value or principal amount upon which the CDS contract is based. This is the amount the Seller will compensate the Buyer in the event of a Credit Event.

Premium/Spread: The regular payment made by the Buyer to the Seller over the life of the CDS contract. It is often expressed as an annualized percentage of the Notional Amount.

Settlement: The process following a Credit Event, where the Seller compensates the Buyer. There are two types of settlements:

  • Physical Settlement: The Buyer delivers the Reference Entity's defaulted debt obligations to the Seller in exchange for the Notional Amount.
  • Cash Settlement: The Seller pays the Buyer an agreed amount based on the market value of the Reference Entity's defaulted debt obligations.

Effective Date: The date on which the CDS contract becomes active and the Buyer’s protection begins.

Termination Date: The date on which the CDS contract expires or ends unless a Credit Event has occurred earlier.

Accrued Premium: The portion of the Premium that has accumulated from the last payment date until the occurrence of a Credit Event or until contract termination.

Credit Derivatives Determinations Committee (CDDC): An industry body that makes binding determinations regarding Credit Events, the Reference Entity, and settlement terms in the case of disputes.

5. Credit Events:

If a Credit Event occurs with respect to the Reference Entity, the Buyer must notify the Seller in writing within [time period] of becoming aware of the event. Valid Credit Events for this contract include:

  • Bankruptcy: The Reference Entity files for or is subjected to bankruptcy proceedings.
  • Failure to Pay: The Reference Entity fails to make a payment on one or more of its obligations when due.
  • Restructuring: The Reference Entity restructures its debt in a manner that negatively impacts creditors.
  • Obligation Acceleration: A debt obligation of the Reference Entity becomes immediately due and payable due to a default or other covenant breach.

6. Settlement Procedure:

Upon the occurrence of a valid Credit Event, the Seller agrees to compensate the Buyer by way of:

  • Physical Settlement: The Buyer delivers debt obligations of the Reference Entity to the Seller, and the Seller pays the Notional Amount in return.
  • Cash Settlement: The Seller will pay the Buyer a cash amount based on the difference between the face value of the Reference Entity’s obligations and their market value post-Credit Event, as determined by the CDDC or market conventions.

7. Accrued Premium:

Upon the occurrence of a Credit Event, the Buyer will pay any Accrued Premium that has accumulated up until the Credit Event date. This amount will be deducted from any payout under the settlement terms.

The Termination Date is [Termination Date], unless a Credit Event occurs prior to this date, in which case the contract will terminate upon settlement.

9. Dispute Resolution:

Any disputes regarding the occurrence of a Credit Event or the terms of the contract will be referred to the Credit Derivatives Determinations Committee (CDDC) for binding resolution.

10. Events of Default by Buyer:

The Seller reserves the right to terminate the contract or seek damages if:

  • The Buyer fails to make the Premium payments as required under the contract.
  • The Buyer misrepresents material facts relating to the Reference Entity.
